Data fetching might be slow if connection to SMHI servers are slow

Mark the day you wish to download data for in the calendar. Days with light blue colour have data. Radar images for chosen day will be displayed in the map. There are images for every 5 minutes. You can play or step through the day's images with the controlls under the map.

Units

Radarreflectivity (Z) is most often shown logarithmically and is then asigned the unit dBZ. SMHI converts Z to rainfall intensity (R i mm/h) with the folowing formula:

Z=10log10(200R1.5)

Usually, reflections lower than 5 dBZ aren't treated as rainfall (it can be birds, insects or other reflecting things), while reflections higher than 70 dBZ are less likely to occur in northern climats. Below is a table with some values converted from dBZ to mm/h.
